Culture & Expectations

WORKING
WITH US

Wholesome Plates is a professional kitchen, not a stepping stone. If you join this team, we expect you to care — about the food, the people, and the standard. Here is exactly what that looks like.

The Standard

SIX
NON-NEGOTIABLES

These are not aspirational values on a wall. They are the baseline — the minimum expectation for everyone on this team, regardless of role or tenure.

01

Takes Direction Without Ego

Feedback is fuel here, not criticism. The team that listens and adapts performs at a higher level than the one that already knows everything. Rank has no bearing on whether you're open to correction.

02

Pursues Improvement

We expect everyone — from prep to lead — to be actively getting better. Ask questions. Study the craft. Do your work slightly better today than you did yesterday. Stagnation is not an option here.

03

Kitchen Cleanliness Is Always the Standard

A clean kitchen is a safe kitchen. A safe kitchen is a professional kitchen. This is not a preference — it is a non-negotiable from Day 1 through every day after.

04

Integrity

Do what you said you would do, especially when no one is watching. We trust our team completely until given a reason not to. That trust is earned and it is yours to protect.

05

Proactive Communication

If something is off, say something. If you are going to be late, call ahead. If you see a problem forming, raise it before it becomes a crisis. Silence is not neutral — it is costly.

06

Ownership Mindset

Treat this kitchen like it belongs to you. That means caring about quality, watching the details, and not waiting to be told before handling something obvious. Leaders are made here, not assigned.

Your Journey

THREE PHASES
OF GROWTH

Phase One
1

Foundation

You are here to learn the system and prove you belong. Focus on showing up consistently, mastering your station, and executing our standards without shortcuts. Success at this phase looks like reliability, cleanliness, and a readiness to absorb everything.

Months 1 – 3
Phase Two
2

Contribution

You have earned trust. Now we expect more. Run your station without reminders, support newer team members, and proactively improve the work around you. We will give you more responsibility as you demonstrate readiness — not before, and never after.

Months 4 – 9
Phase Three
3

Leadership

If you have excelled through Phases 1 and 2, we will invest in your advancement. This may look like a senior role, lead position at events, or a defined path toward kitchen management. Growth here is earned — and it is real.

Month 10+
Our Commitment

WHAT TO EXPECT
FROM LEADERSHIP

Clear Expectations

You will always know what is expected of you. We do not move goalposts or hold back information that affects your performance. Clarity is a form of respect.

Honest Feedback

We will tell you what is working and what is not, directly and with respect. No surprises at review time. No feedback withheld until it is too late to act on.

Real Investment

If you are committed to growth, we are committed to your development. That includes training, mentorship, and real advancement opportunities — not empty promises.

Fair Treatment

You will be paid fairly, treated with dignity, and heard when you speak. This is non-negotiable from leadership — the same way it is non-negotiable from you.

Mutual Accountability

We hold ourselves to the same standards we ask of you. Leadership models the culture here — it does not just manage it. You will never be held to a standard we are not willing to keep ourselves.

From the Founder

"Wholesome Plates is being built to last. The people inside it are what make that possible. If these values align with how you already show up to work, you will find a home here. If they are where you want to grow, we want to help you get there. We do not expect perfection. We expect alignment, drive, and honesty. Everything else we can build together."

Ariana Wharton
Founder & CEO, Wholesome Plates Catering LLC
